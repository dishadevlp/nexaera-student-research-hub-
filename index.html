About Nexaera

Nexaera – Student Research Hub is a student-founded research community dedicated to fostering innovation, collaboration, and knowledge sharing. Our mission is to provide a platform for students from various disciplines to come together, explore new ideas, and drive meaningful impact.

Empower

We unleash the potential of individuals and communities by providing the tools, resources, and connections needed to turn innovative ideas into impactful research.

Elevate

We amplify diverse perspectives and voices in the research community, ensuring that every student has the opportunity to contribute to meaningful discoveries.

Envision

We shape the future through collaborative research and innovation, bringing together bright minds to solve the world's most pressing challenges.

Our Story

Nexaera started as a small idea driven by curiosity,the question of how students can move beyond just studying and begin creating, researching, and contributing to real-world conversations. It began with exploring topics like sustainability, economics, and future careers, and slowly grew into a platform focused on learning by doing. Instead of waiting for opportunities, Nexaera was built to create them, through projects, collaborations, and sharing knowledge with others. Our story is about experimenting, learning from mistakes, and building something meaningful step by step. Nexaera is still evolving, shaped by the belief that progress starts with initiative and consistency, not perfection.

Ruppee's fall is real

Is the rupee really falling… or is this just another headline panic? At Nexaera Student Research Hub , we looked beyond the USD–INR chart and found something more interesting. The rupee’s recent weakness isn’t limited to the dollar, it’s depreciating against the euro, pound, yen, and yuan too. When both NEER and REER show a downward trend, it signals something deeper than short-term volatility. High domestic inflation, shifting global capital flows, and rising intervention pressures are shaping a real (inflation-adjusted) depreciation, with implications for exports, imports, and macroeconomic stability.
